How to Get Your Apple ID Password

If you've forgotten your Apple ID password, follow these steps to recover or reset it:

  • Use Apple's official password reset tool at iforgot.apple.com
  • Answer your security questions if you set them up
  • Use two-factor authentication if enabled on your account
  • Check your trusted devices for a password reset notification
  • Look for an email from Apple with password reset instructions
Security Tip: Always make sure you're on an official Apple website (apple.com) before entering any account information. Apple will never ask for your password via email or phone.

Apple ID Password Recovery Options

1

Using a Trusted Device

If you have two-factor authentication enabled, you can reset your password directly from a trusted iPhone, iPad, or Mac. Go to Settings > [your name] > Password & Security > Change Password.

2

Using Account Recovery

If you can't access any trusted devices, use account recovery at iforgot.apple.com. This process may take a few days to verify your identity and ensure account security.

3

With Security Questions

If you set up security questions, you can answer them to reset your password. Visit iforgot.apple.com and select "Answer security questions" when prompted.

Password Help & Support

Having trouble with your Apple ID password?

  • Forgot Password: Use Apple's password reset tool at iforgot.apple.com
  • Account Locked: Wait 24 hours or verify your identity to unlock
  • Security Questions: Contact Apple Support if you can't remember your answers
  • Two-Factor Issues: Make sure you have access to a trusted device or phone number
  • Phishing Attempts: Never share your password via email or phone calls
Remember: Your Apple ID password gives access to all Apple services including iCloud, App Store, and device backups. Keep it secure and don't share it with anyone.
